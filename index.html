Getting Started

When you first open GradeSight Pro, you will see the Welcome Screen asking if you are a GWH staff member. Click "Yes, I'm a GWH Teacher".

You will then be asked if you have registered before. New teachers should click "No, I'm new here" and create a username and password. Your username is typically your first and last name combined (e.g. priyasharma). The app will suggest a shorter version like ps@GWH — click it to use it.

Returning teachers click "Yes, I've registered" and log in with their username and password.

After logging in for the first time, you will be taken to the Profile Setup screen. Fill in your name, the subjects you teach, and map each subject to its class sections. For example, if you teach Mathematics to classes 8A and 9B, add "Mathematics" as a subject and then type "8A" and "9B" in the sections box for that subject. If you are a class teacher, enter your class section (e.g. 8A) in the Class Teacher field.

⚠ Important: After setting up your profile, immediately click "Download Backup" at the bottom of the sidebar. Store this file safely in Google Drive or a USB drive. If you ever clear your browser data, you will need this file to recover your account.
📊

Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen. It shows a summary of your students, subjects, sections, and upcoming events at a glance.

The stat cards at the top show total students, subjects you teach, assigned sections, and upcoming events. Below them, the class-subject chips show every class-subject pair you teach.

The Countdown widget shows how many days are left until your next upcoming event. The Timetable Widget shows today's schedule. The Top Scorers card highlights the highest-performing student in each exam for each class.

To edit your profile (name, subjects, sections), scroll to the bottom of the sidebar and click "Edit Profile".

🗓

Timetable

Go to the Timetable section from the sidebar. Click "Build Timetable" to start. Choose the number of periods per day using the input at the top, then click "Build Grid".

A grid of days and periods will appear. On the right, you will see coloured blocks for each of your subjects and sections. Drag a block from the palette onto any cell in the grid to place it. To move a block, drag it from one cell to another. Double-click a placed block to remove it.

To rename a block (for example, to add a class name like "Mathematics - 8A"), hover over the placed block and click the small pencil icon that appears in the top-right corner of the cell.

When done, click "Save Timetable". The saved timetable can be Printed or Copied as an Image using the buttons above it — useful for sharing with students or pinning on a notice board.

📅

Calendar & Events

The Calendar section shows a monthly and a weekly view. Days with events have small coloured dots beneath them. Click any date to see events for that day.

To add an event, click the "+ Add Event" button or click a date and then "Add event for this day". Fill in the event name, select a category (Exam, Holiday, Meeting, Activity, or Other), pick the date and time, and optionally add notes.

The colour of the dot on the calendar matches the category — indigo for Exams, green for Holidays, amber for Meetings, pink for Activities. You can filter events in the "All Events" panel by category using the filter pills.

Events that have passed are shown at reduced opacity in the All Events list.

👩‍🎓

Students

Go to the Students section. The General tab shows all students as coloured cards. The Select Class dropdown only shows the classes you teach.

To add a student, click "Add Student", enter their name and roll number, and click Add. To add many students at once, click "Import CSV". Download the template, fill it in with names and roll numbers, and upload it. You can also paste CSV data directly into the text box.

To view a student's profile — which shows their overall average, best and weakest subjects, attendance percentage, a performance trend chart, and a breakdown of all four exams — click their coloured card. To remove a student, open their profile and click the red "Remove" button in the top-right corner of the modal.

The Focused View panel at the top of the Students section lets you filter the marks view to a specific class and subject.

📝

Entering Marks

In the Students section, click the exam tabs: Unit Test 1, Term 1, Unit Test 2, or Term 2 (Final). Select a class from the dropdown to load the marks table.

Click any cell in the table and type the mark. The maximum for Unit Tests is 25 and for Terms is 80. If you enter a value above the maximum, the cell turns red and the mark is not saved — correct it first.

Class teachers can see and enter marks for all subjects. Subject teachers can only enter marks for the subjects they teach.

Once marks are entered, the stats bar below the table shows the Class Topper, Class Average, and student count. Click "Export CSV" to download the marks as a spreadsheet, or "Print" to open a clean printable report with grade calculations.

✅

Attendance

The Attendance section is accessible from the sidebar. Select a class and make sure you are in Daily mode. Each student appears as a coloured card.

To mark a student Present, click the green ✓ button at the bottom-left of their card. The card fills with a green fluid sweep and glows green. To mark Absent, click the red ✗ button at the bottom-right. Clicking the same button again clears the mark. A notification appears at the bottom-left of the screen confirming the action.

Use the ‹ › arrows or the date picker to navigate between dates. The "All Present" and "All Absent" buttons mark every student at once.

Double-click a student card to open their Attendance Summary — which shows overall percentage, monthly breakdown, and lets you set a custom date range or quickly jump to This Month, This Year, or the Academic Year.

The Monthly Summary tab shows all students sorted by lowest attendance first, making it easy to identify students at risk.

To define Holidays (which are excluded from attendance calculations), click the 🗓 Holidays tab. By default, Saturdays and Sundays are marked as holidays. Add named date ranges (e.g. Summer Break: April 1 – June 2), single days (e.g. Diwali), or recurring weekly holidays (e.g. every Friday).

💾

Backup & Data Safety

All your data is saved in your browser's local storage. It survives closing the browser, restarting the computer, and rebooting — but it is removed if you clear your browser's site data.

To protect your data, regularly click "Download Backup" at the bottom of the sidebar. This downloads a GradeSight_backup_username_date.json file containing all your credentials and app data.

If you ever lose your data (e.g. after clearing the browser), go to the login screen and click "Restore from Backup File" in the yellow warning box. Select your backup file, enter your password, and all your data will be restored.

💡 Tip: Save your backup file to Google Drive so it is always accessible even if your computer is replaced.

About GradeSight Pro

GradeSight Pro was built out of a very real frustration — watching teachers juggle spreadsheets, paper mark sheets, and scattered Excel Sheets just to manage their classrooms. The goal from day one was simple: bring everything a Greenwood High teacher needs into a single, fast, beautiful interface that actually gets out of the way and lets them teach.

Every feature was designed around real classroom workflows. The exam tabs mirror the school's actual assessment structure — Unit Tests and Terms. The timetable builder reflects the way periods are actually planned. The countdown timer keeps teachers aware of upcoming events without having to check a separate calendar app.

Class teachers — the ones responsible for an entire section — get a special 👑 Crown Mode that unlocks marks entry across every subject for their class, not just the subjects they personally teach. The platform then does the heavy lifting: calculating totals, percentages, class averages, and surfacing the top performer automatically.
